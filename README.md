# Student Habit & Performance Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes students' lifestyle and study habits to understand their impact on exam performance. The dataset includes factors such as study hours, digital usage, sleep, exercise, caffeine intake, and mental health. Using Excel analysis, the project identifies patterns related to focus, burnout, productivity, and exam scores. The goal is to understand which habits help students perform better in exams. 

---

## Data Cleaning

The dataset was cleaned using the following steps:

* Removed duplicate records
* Standardized text format
* Replaced incorrect values
* Created calculated columns
* Converted minutes to hours
* Rounded numeric values

## Key Insights

* Students who study **more than 10 hours** tend to achieve higher exam scores.
* As **digital screen time increases**, exam scores gradually decrease.
* Students with **low burnout** show higher productivity and exam scores.
* Students who sleep **7–8 hours or more** have better focus and productivity.
* Exam performance differences across gender groups are **small**, with similar trends.
* About **57% of students fall in middle burnout**, **39% in high burnout**, and **3% in low burnout**.
* Students with **deadlines and part-time work** tend to experience higher burnout and lower exam scores.

## Conclusion

This project analyzes how student lifestyle habits affect academic performance. The analysis shows that **study hours, digital usage, sleep patterns, and burnout levels strongly influence exam scores**. Students who maintain balanced study time, moderate digital usage, proper sleep, and low burnout tend to achieve better focus, productivity, and higher exam performance. The dashboard helps visualize these relationships and provides useful insights into student behavior.
